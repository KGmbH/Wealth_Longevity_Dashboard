# Wealth & Longevity Dashboard

An interactive data dashboard built with Quarto and R, investigating whether economic growth translates into longer lives across 142 countries from 1952 to 2007.

## View the dashboard
👉 [Click here to view the dashboard](https://kgmbh.github.io/Wealth_Longevity_Dashboard/Wealth_Longevity.html)

## What's inside
- **Overview** — Global trends in life expectancy, GDP and population from 1952 to 2007
- **Growth by Continent** — How progress differed across Africa, Americas, Asia, Europe and Oceania
- **Does Money Buy Health?** — The relationship between GDP and life expectancy, and how it evolved over time
- **Outliers: Who Bucks the Trend?** — Countries that deviate significantly from the global wealth-health pattern
- **Why Are They Sicker?** — Correlates of unexpectedly low life expectancy: HIV, tuberculosis and sanitation access

## Key findings
- Global life expectancy rose from 49 to 67 years between 1952 and 2007
- Africa consistently lagged behind despite overall global progress
- Within Africa, the GDP-health correlation declined sharply from the 1980s — likely driven by the HIV/AIDS epidemic
- Countries with unexpectedly low life expectancy fall into two groups: disease-driven (high HIV/TB) and sanitation-driven (poor infrastructure)

## Built with
- [Quarto](https://quarto.org/) — dashboard framework
- [R](https://www.r-project.org/) — data wrangling and analysis
- [Gapminder](https://www.gapminder.org/) — core dataset
- [World Bank WDI](https://databank.worldbank.org/) — additional health indicators
